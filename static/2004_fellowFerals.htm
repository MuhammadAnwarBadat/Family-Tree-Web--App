fellow ferals

11/19/04

Saturday afternoon, Olivier comes over for a play date with Max.
After a couple of hours of shouting, arguing, wrestling, throwing,
food messes, squalling guitars and other testosterone
I say let's get outta here, plz?
Max takes his little training wheel bike and rides round the block.
Olivier and I walk/run along side him scraping, laughing.
We park the bike around the corner by a building with a wizened strip of grass
the boys love to play on.
The building doorman tries to chase them off like feral dogs
while I coax them to stay and play, a feral dog spawning dogs.
I guess the Man dozes this Saturday because they play unfettered for awhile.
Then they kneel down by a wire fence near an alley
scraping runes in the dirt
talking quietly with the intimacy of little boys.

It turns cold by late afternoon --
the boys, ungloved, jackets open, glow red hope
but for me, it seeps to my bones -
standing still on the sidwalk
listening little to the little boys whisper
and watching
the wind dance with the maple trees
and the dusk gather over 110th.


-rsharpe, 2004
